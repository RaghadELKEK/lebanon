Attractions in Lebanon

Beirut Waterfront

The Beirut Waterfront, located along the Mediterranean Sea, is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. It offers stunning views of the sea and is lined with restaurants, cafes, and shops. Visitors can take leisurely strolls along the promenade, enjoy delicious seafood, or simply relax and soak in the vibrant atmosphere.

Baalbek Ruins

The Baalbek Ruins, located in the Bekaa Valley, are an impressive archaeological site dating back to Roman times. The site is home to some of the largest and best-preserved Roman ruins in the world, including the Temple of Bacchus and the Temple of Jupiter. Visitors can explore the ancient complex, marvel at the grandeur of the structures, and learn about the history of the region.

Jeita Grotto

Located in the Nahr al-Kalb valley, the Jeita Grotto is a fascinating natural wonder. It consists of two separate but interconnected limestone caves, filled with stunning stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can take a boat ride on the underground river to explore the lower cave, followed by a walk through the upper cave, marveling at the breathtaking formations along the way.
